Our Solar System

Description


The solar system is the planetary system that gravitationally binds a set of astronomical objects that rotate directly or indirectly in an orbit around a single star known as the Sun.

The solar system was formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the collapse of a molecular cloud. The residual material originated a protoplanetary circumstellar disk in which the physical processes that led to the formation of the planets occurred.


SUN

Description


The Sun is a G-type star of the main sequence and luminosity class V located in the center of the solar system and constitutes the largest source of electromagnetic radiation in this planetary system.​

• It is in the center of the Solar System
• It's a hot shiny ball
• Composed of hydrogen and helium
• Its diameter is 1,392,000 km
• It is considered a star
• It is approximately 4,500,000.00 years old
• Planets and other bodies orbit the sun
• Its visibility in the sky determines the day and night


MERCURY

Description


Mercury is the planet in the solar system closest to the Sun and the smallest. It is part of the so-called inner or terrestrial planets and lacks natural satellites like Venus.

• It is the smallest planet in the solar system
• It is the closest to the sun but not the hottest
• It is a rocky planet, with a solid surface full of craters.
• It has no moon and has a thin atmosphere
• Mercury only has one sunrise every 180 Earth days
• The surface features numerous meteorite impacts


VENUS

Description


Venus is the second planet in the solar system in order of distance from the Sun, the sixth in terms of size, ordered from largest to smallest. Like Mercury, it lacks natural satellites.

• It is the hottest planet
• It has a dense atmosphere, full of carbon dioxide that causes a greenhouse effect
• It has mountains and volcanoes
• It has no moons
• It rotates in the opposite direction to planet Earth
• Its rotation is slow
• The sun rises in the west and sets in the east
• The Sun rises every 117 Earth days


EARTH

Description


Earth was formed approximately 4.55 billion years ago, and life arose about a billion years later. It is home to millions of species, including humans, and currently the only astronomical body where life is known to exist.

• It is the only planet in the Solar System from which we know life
• Terrestrial and rocky planet
• Presents a solid surface with mountains, valleys, plains
• 70% of the surface is made up of water
• Known as the blue planet
• A day lasts 24 hours
• A year lasts 365.25 days, 0.25 represents that every 4 years we add a day to the calendar
• It has a natural satellite, Luna


EARTH'S MOON

Description


The Moon is the only natural satellite of the Earth. With an equatorial diameter of 3476 km, it is the fifth largest satellite in the solar system, while in terms of the proportional size with respect to its planet it is the largest satellite: a quarter of the diameter of the Earth and 1/81 of its mass


MARS

Description


Mars is the fourth planet in order of distance from the Sun and the second smallest in the solar system, after Mercury. It received its name in homage to the god of war in Roman mythology (Ares in Greek mythology), and is also known as
"the red planet" due to the reddish appearance that the predominant iron oxide gives it on its surface.

• It's cold and desert
• It is also called the red planet
• Has rusty iron on its surface
• It has volcanoes without activity
• It has a thin atmosphere composed of carbon dioxide, argon and nitrogen
• It has two moons, called: Phobos and Deimos
• There is evidence of salty water on the surface
• It has the largest volcano in the solar system


JUPTER

Description


Jupiter is the fifth planet in the solar system. It is part of the so-called outer or gaseous planets. It gets its name from the Roman god Jupiter (Zeus in Greek mythology).
It is the planet that offers the greatest brightness throughout the year depending on its phase. It is also, after the Sun, the largest celestial body in the solar system, with a mass almost two and a half times that of the other planets combined.
(with a mass 318 times greater than that of Earth and three times greater than that of Saturn, in addition to being, in terms of volume, 1,321 times greater than Earth)

• The biggest planet
• Covered by swirling clouds that appear as stripes
• It presents strong storms that last hundreds of years
• It is a gas giant, they do not have a solid surface
• Composed of hydrogen and helium
• Has rings but too faint to notice
• A day lasts 10 hours and a year is equivalent to 118 years on Earth
• It has 50 confirmed moons


SATURN

Description


Saturn is the sixth planet in the solar system counting from the Sun, the second in size and mass after Jupiter, and the only one with a ring system visible from Earth. Its name comes from the Roman god Saturn.
It is part of the so-called outer or gaseous planets. The most characteristic aspect of Saturn is its bright rings. Before the invention of the telescope, Saturn was the most distant of the known planets and, to the naked eye, it did not appear bright or interesting

• This planet contains seven rings
• The rings are made of pieces of helium and rock
• Saturn is a planet made up of hydrogen and helium
• It has approximately 53 moons
• One day lasts 10.7 hours
• One year equals 29.5 Earth years


URANUS

Description


Uranus is the seventh planet in the solar system, the third largest, and the fourth most massive. It is named in honor of the Greek divinity of the sky Uranus (from the ancient Greek Οὐρανός), the father of Cronus (Saturn) and the grandfather of Zeus (Jupiter).
Although it is detectable with the naked eye in the night sky, it was not listed as a planet by ancient astronomers due to its low luminosity and slow orbit.

• It has 27 moons
• Uranus rotates sideways, so it rotates differently from the rest of the planets
• Features 13 wispy rings
• The inner rings are dark and narrow
• The outer rings are brightly colored
• It is composed of water, methane and ammonia
• Its atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium and methane
• Methane is what gives the color blue
• A day lasts seventeen hours and a year is equivalent to 84 Earth years


NEPTUNE

Description


Neptune is the eighth planet in distance from the Sun and the farthest in the solar system. It is part of the so-called outer planets, and within these, it is one of the frozen giants, and it is the first to be discovered thanks to mathematical predictions.
Its name was placed in honor of the Roman god of the sea - Neptune - and it is the fourth planet in diameter and the third largest in mass. Its mass is seventeen times that of Earth and slightly greater than that of its "twin" planet Uranus, which has 15 Earth masses and is not as dense.

• It's dark, cold and very windy
• It is composed of water, ammonia and methane
• Its atmosphere is made up of hydrogen, helium and methane
• Features six hard-to-see rings
• One day lasts 16 hours, one year lasts 164 years of Planet Earth
• Features 13 moons
